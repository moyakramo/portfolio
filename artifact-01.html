Introduction

A single-source visual reference tracing the development of artificial intelligence and machine learning across five distinct eras, from Turing's foundational work to today's generative and agentic systems, with emphasis on the two AI winters and the hardware breakthroughs that ended them. It follows how machine learning grew from an early idea into the engine behind modern AI.

Objective

To create a clear, credible primer for quickly understanding where AI has been, why the field cycles through booms and busts, and what the current landscape actually looks like beyond the headlines.

The Timeline

If the embedded viewer doesn't load on your device, open the PDF in a new tab.

Process

Researched three assigned sources (IEEE Computer Society, Our World in Data, and a documentary history of AI). Cross-referenced dates and figures across all three to verify accuracy. Structured milestones into five thematic eras.

Tools & Technologies

PowerPoint · Claude (Anthropic) for research organization and structural review · PDF export.

Value Proposition

For finance and business audiences facing constant AI hype, this timeline strips out the noise and delivers a five-minute grounding in the field's actual history. It shows why today's boom is different from past cycles, which companies actually matter, and what "narrow AI" vs "AGI" really means, enabling smarter conversations, sharper questions, and better decisions about AI investment or adoption.

Key Learnings

  • Historical accuracy and visual clarity often pull in opposite directions.
  • The AI winters are the most valuable part of this story for a business audience.
  • Cross-referencing sources surfaced small inconsistencies that would have gone unnoticed with a single source.
